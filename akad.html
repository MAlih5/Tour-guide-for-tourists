The Akkadian Empire

The Akkadian Empire (c. 2334–2154 BCE) was the first ancient empire of Mesopotamia, founded by Sargon of Akkad. It united the Sumerian city-states under a centralized rule and expanded to encompass much of Mesopotamia, becoming a model for future empires in the region.

Scientific and Artistic Achievements

Akkadian Language

[Place for Akkadian Text Image]

The Akkadians popularized the use of their Semitic language, which became the lingua franca of Mesopotamia for centuries.

Artistic Innovations

[Place for Artistic Innovations Image]

Developed dynamic and narrative art styles, as seen in the Victory Stele of Naram-Sin.

Seals and Inscriptions

[Place for Seal Image]

Enhanced administrative efficiency with the use of inscribed cylinder seals and detailed records.
